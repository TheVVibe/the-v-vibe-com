The harvest season is upon us! As the sun changes its course and the skies fill with colorful kites, it’s time to welcome Makar Sankranti. While this festival is known for sesame sweets (Til-Gud), vibrant decorations, and joyous gatherings, for fashion enthusiasts, it means one very specific, very elegant thing: The Black Saree.

Usually, black is avoided during auspicious Indian occasions. However, Makar Sankranti is the beautiful exception.

## Why Wear Black on Sankranti?

Tradition holds that since Sankranti falls on one of the last cold days of winter, black is worn to absorb heat and keep warm. Culturally, it is also believed that wearing black on this specific day wards off evil eyes (drishti) as you step out to celebrate.

This year, let’s honor this beautiful tradition without compromising on contemporary style. Here is your guide to rocking the black saree this Sankranti, along with the perfect accessory trends to complete the look.

## The Main Event: The Black Saree
A black saree is like a blank canvas—it can be traditional, modern, sultry, or sophisticated depending on how you style it. Here are two trending ways to wear it this season, with my top picks for you to shop.
